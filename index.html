2020

HackwithHer 3.0

Chitkara University in collaboration with IEEE CIET is organizing Hack With Her 3.0, a coding and hardware national level hackathon. Bringing a splendid opportunity for you to turn your ideas to codes along with knowledge regarding various hardware components.

CODEATHON

Competitive Coding Showdown

The Codeathon will be a “Competitive Programming Hackathon” and will focus on the technical skills of the participants. It will be a 24 hours competition, in which participants will be given some problems, and judged on the basis of their speed and efficiency.

Rules

  • Participants must bring their own laptops.
  • Teams must consist of 2-3 students.
  • Extra benefits for teams with a female participant(s).
  • 24 hours will be given in total to code.
  • Problems use Data Structures, Algos, Inheritance, Bitwise Operators and other concepts.

MAKEATHON

Theme Based Hackathon

This event will be a 2-day prototyping competition. Each team is assigned a particular theme and, using the available hardware, the teams prepare a prototype while ensuring that it satisfies all the specified requirements. The prototype may be working or a mockup. The judgment criteria include creativity, design and innovation.

Rules

  • Participants are requested to bring their own hardware (as needed).
  • Teams must consist of 2-3 students.
  • Extra benefits for teams with a female participant(s).
  • Each team has a fixed duration to come up with the final prototype.
  • Certain hardware will be made available to you on the spot. Information regarding this will be updated later.

Got Questions? Here's some answered

Helping clear some queries so you can focus on having fun!

What is the team size?
2-3 students can group up per team.
What if I feel my hack isn't good enough?
Unimpressive projects (that's what you think) should be presented too! Showcasing your hack will boost your confidence and judges can give perspective of where you can improve.
Are there prizes for all girl teams?
To promote and encourage equal gender participation we have special prizes just for girls!
Can I start working on my project already?
No, students should not be working on their projects before the event begins. However, you can plan before with the team and get to know the technologies you intend to use.
